## Theory of change

A blended project: a community-marine-trail tourism operation (commercial channel — paying eco-tourists from a partner resort) cross-subsidises a school-based marine education programme + mangrove nursery (public-good channel — free for local children and fishermen). An Anchor Partner — the fictional Coastal Resilience Foundation — provides JMD $1.8M/year for 3 years to close the gap on the public-good channel. The intervention restores 3 hectares of degraded mangrove fringe along the Hanover north coast, increases nursery-fish habitat, creates a tourism livelihood for 10 cooperative members, and delivers marine-literacy education to 280 children at Riverside Primary School.

## Pathway Engine canvas — populated

### Block 1: Key Partners

**Commercial + Anchor layer (CVP + Anchor)**
> ★ **ANCHOR PARTNER**: Coastal Resilience Foundation (Letter of Intent signed April 2026; Memorandum of Understanding in drafting — target signing 30 September 2026; 3-year cash commitment of JMD $1.8M/year). Commercial partners: Blue Mountain Inn Hanover (eco-tourist offtake — 25% of trail visitors); Coral Bay Restaurant (catered tour meals); Island Seedling Cooperative (mangrove propagule supplier).

**Impact layer (IVP)**
> East Bay Community Trust (Stewarding Community Body — recipient of the public-good portion; legally registered with appointed board); Riverside Primary School (lead school partner — 280 children, 6 teachers); RADA Hanover office (technical advisor on mangrove restoration); Coastal Conservation Alliance (NGO advising on IUCN NbS Global Standard); EFJ Programme Officer.

### Block 2: Key Activities

**Commercial + Cross-Subsidy layer**
> Commercial: 4 paid eco-tourism trail tours per week (45 tourists/week peak season, 25/week shoulder season); cross-subsidy accounting tagged at the ratio 55% commercial / 45% public good across every cost line; quarterly cross-subsidy report to the Anchor Partner (Coastal Resilience Foundation) showing both flows; monthly Blue Mountain Inn invoice.

**Impact layer**
> Restore 3 hectares of mangrove fringe along the Hanover north coast (Year 1: nursery + planting; Year 2: monitoring); MRV monthly using community-collected data + drone canopy photography; 12 community stewards trained in mangrove monitoring; monthly community report-back to East Bay Community Trust; GBV-safe site protocol with referral pathway to Hanover Police Domestic Violence Intervention Centre; school-based marine education for 280 Riverside Primary School children (12 sessions per year).

### Block 3: Value Proposition

**Commercial value proposition**
> Eco-tourists pay JMD $4,500 per person for a 2-hour guided mangrove canoe tour + marine education + cooperative-made meal — the only certified marine-resilience tour in west Hanover. ESG-aligned tourism storyline used by Blue Mountain Inn in guest welcome packets and on their marketing site. The Anchor Partner (Coastal Resilience Foundation) sponsors because their charter requires investing in parish-level climate resilience and their board scores them on demonstrable adaptation outcomes.

**Impact value proposition**
> Riverside Primary School-feeding programme + marine education curriculum receives 12 sessions per year free of charge; 280 children with improved marine literacy + 50 children with improved nutrition; 3 hectares of mangrove restored = flood buffer for the low-lying East Bay village (population ~480); contribution to Jamaica's NDC adaptation target on coastal ecosystem restoration.

### Block 4: Customer / Stewardship Relationship

**Commercial + Anchor layer**
> Commercial: quarterly impact stories shared with Blue Mountain Inn ESG team (used in guest packets); 12-month tour-offtake agreement with Blue Mountain Inn covering 25% of expected trail capacity; Anchor Partner: quarterly dashboard report by email + annual in-person review meeting with Coastal Resilience Foundation board; signed MoU reviewed annually.

**Impact layer**
> Annual community report-back showing the 45% public-good delivery actually achieved (sessions delivered + children attended + mangrove hectares planted); Riverside Primary School principal sits on the project board; grievance pathway operated via the Hanover parish council with publicly-posted SLA (response within 14 days).

### Block 5: Customer / Beneficiary Segment

**Commercial + Anchor layer**
> Commercial paying customers: 1,200 eco-tourists per year (mix of resort guests + day visitors). Anchor Partner: Coastal Resilience Foundation (cash co-funder for 3 years). Cross-subsidy ratio: 55% commercial / 45% public good.

**Impact layer**
> 280 children at Riverside Primary School (marine education + nutrition); 50 children with improved nutrition (subset receiving school feeding); 48 elderly residents in the East Bay catchment (reduced flood exposure); 70% women-headed households among the 110 East Bay catchment families; 8 Persons with Disabilities in the catchment; restored mangrove fringe supporting nursery-fish populations used by local fishermen.

### Block 6: Key Resources

**Commercial + Anchor layer**
> 3-hectare mangrove fringe right-of-use (25-year agreement with parish council); 4 fibreglass canoes + 12 PFDs (life jackets); booking + payment infrastructure; 'Hanover Marine Trail' brand registered; co-branded marketing with Coastal Resilience Foundation. Signed Anchor Partner MoU with named contact (Coastal Resilience Foundation Programme Manager) + standard quarterly reporting template.

**Impact layer**
> 12,000 mangrove propagule inventory (sourced from Island Seedling Cooperative); IUCN-aligned technical advisor on retainer; East Bay Community Trust as named CBO partner (signed 5-year stewardship agreement); M&E system with monthly community-collected data + quarterly external verification by RADA.

### Block 7: Channels

**Commercial + Anchor layer**
> (i) Commercial: tours run Tuesday + Thursday + Saturday; bookings via the Hanover Marine Trail web page + Blue Mountain Inn concierge; daily marketing posts on Instagram. (ii) Public-good: Riverside Primary School sessions Wednesday + Friday afternoons. (iii) Anchor: quarterly dashboard email report to Coastal Resilience Foundation + annual in-person meeting.

**Impact layer**
> Quarterly community meeting at East Bay village hall; Coastal Resilience Foundation public impact dashboard (linked from project website); school assembly talks at Riverside Primary twice a year; annual impact storytelling event during Hanover Heritage Week.

### Block 8: Cost Structure

**Commercial + Anchor layer**
> Total budget JMD $12.6M. Commercial-tagged: 55% = $6.93M (canoes, staff, marketing, customer service, processing fees). Public-good-tagged: 45% = $5.67M (school sessions, community engagement, MRV, mangrove planting, in-kind valued labour). Administrative: $880K (7%). Cross-subsidy ratio reviewed annually with the Anchor Partner; reported in the annual community report.

**Impact layer**
> MRV: $220K/year. IUCN NbS self-assessment: $90K (Year 1 only). Community engagement: $280K/year. GBV safeguards: $60K.

### Block 9: Revenue Streams / Sustainability Inflows

**Commercial + Anchor layer**
> Commercial sales: eco-tourism tours JMD $4.5M/year (55% of inflows). ★ Anchor Partner cross-subsidy: $1.8M/year for 3 years (signed MoU); restaurant cost contribution from Coral Bay: $200K/year. Optional carbon credits Year 4+ from mangrove (blue carbon): projected $400K/year.

**Impact layer**
> Community in-kind: $1.0M/year (East Bay Community Trust labour + Riverside Primary School teacher time, valued at replacement cost). Implicit fiscal saving to Hanover parish council from mangrove flood-buffer service (replacing storm-drain clearance work): $600K/year. Year 3 follow-on EFJ cycle: $1.5M.

## Final reflection (Stage 6 — fictional learner reflection)

> "The Anchor Partner Mirage trap caught me. I had been telling myself we 'had' Coastal Resilience Foundation as the anchor, but a signed Letter of Intent is not a Memorandum of Understanding — the cross-subsidy structure I designed only works if the MoU is binding. The amber T2 result was a useful slap. The other surprise was the implicit fiscal saving to the parish council from the storm-drain reduction — I had not been counting that as an inflow, but it is a real, defensible, in-kind credit and adds $600K/year to my sustainability story."
