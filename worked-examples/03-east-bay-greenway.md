## Theory of change

A registered community trust partners with Clarendon Parish Council to install an Urban Greenway — 1.2 hectares of public tree canopy + 600 m of bioswales (vegetated stormwater channels) — through the low-lying East Bay neighbourhood of Clarendon. The intervention reduces stormwater flooding by an estimated 70%, lowers neighbourhood temperatures by 2-3°C, restores a degraded perennial stream, and creates a safer walking corridor. The Parish Council commits via Memorandum of Understanding (currently in drafting) to adopt the Greenway into their fiscal-year 2027 operations budget at JMD $1.2M/year. The East Bay Community Trust stewards day-to-day management. No paying customers — the Adopting Agency budget line + community in-kind labour are the financial sustainability mechanisms.

## Pathway Engine canvas — populated

### Block 1: Key Partners

**Stewardship & Adoption layer (AVP)**
> ★ **ADOPTING AGENCY**: Clarendon Parish Council Climate Office (Memorandum of Understanding in drafting, target signing 30 September 2026; 3-year budget commitment of JMD $1.2M/year from FY 2027). ★ **STEWARDING COMMUNITY**: East Bay Community Trust (legally registered with 7-member elected board; 5-year stewardship agreement signed).

**Impact layer (IVP)**
> NEPA Clarendon office (regulatory approvals for tree-planting + bioswales); RADA Clarendon (technical advisor on native species selection); Riverside Primary School (school partner for outdoor classroom + tree planting events); East Bay Methodist Church (community-meeting host); Clarendon Health Department (heat-stress monitoring data); EFJ Programme Officer; Global Affairs Canada (programme funder).

### Block 2: Key Activities

**Stewardship & Adoption layer**
> Year 1 (grant period): build the NbCS asset with community labour — plant 480 native trees, install 600 m of bioswales, install signage with grievance hotline. Train 6 Clarendon Parish Council technicians on Greenway maintenance + train 12 East Bay Community Trust stewards on monitoring. Hand over to Parish Council budget line at Month 24 with documented operational handbook.

**Impact layer**
> Quarterly canopy + temperature measurements (community-collected, externally verified by RADA); 4 community co-design events per year at East Bay Methodist Church; complete the 5 EFJ-adopted IUCN NbS criteria (mitigation, biodiversity, governance, trade-offs, grant compliance) by Month 6; document GBV referral pathway with Clarendon Police Domestic Violence Intervention Centre and post on Greenway signage; gender-responsive planting schedule (timing community-engagement events to women's availability).

### Block 3: Value Proposition

**Adoption value proposition**
> Clarendon Parish currently spends approximately JMD $4 million per year on stormwater drainage clearance in East Bay (parish council records). The Urban Greenway reduces that recurring cost by an estimated JMD $2.4 million per year (verified by RADA hydrological modelling) — a payback period of 5 years against the $11.2M grant capital. Aligned with the Clarendon Parish Development Order section 4.2 on green infrastructure + Jamaica's NDC adaptation target on urban resilience.

**Impact value proposition**
> Permanent shade across 1.2 hectares of public space (Greenway); 70% reduction in flood depth during heavy rain (modelled); safer walking corridor for women and children + elders (well-lit + monitored); biodiversity-rich micro-habitat for native pollinators + bird species; restored perennial stream supporting downstream agriculture for 14 smallholders.

### Block 4: Customer / Stewardship Relationship

**Stewardship & Adoption layer**
> MoU signed (target) August 2026 with a 3-year budget line in Clarendon Parish Council Fiscal Year 2027 plan; quarterly NbCS performance report submitted to the Parish Council climate committee covering canopy growth, bioswale function, beneficiary feedback, and budget execution.

**Impact layer**
> East Bay Community Trust co-manages from Day 1 + ownership transferred at Month 24; quarterly public consultation at East Bay Methodist Church (open to all 1,800 catchment residents); community ownership transfer ceremony at Month 24 with signed minutes; grievance hotline with publicly-posted Service Level Agreement (response within 14 days, monthly grievance log posted at the Greenway entrance).

### Block 5: Customer / Beneficiary Segment

**Stewardship & Adoption layer**
> ★ ADOPTING AGENCY: Clarendon Parish Council Climate Office (budget allocation + monitoring; decision-maker: Director of Climate Office). ★ STEWARDING COMMUNITY: East Bay Community Trust (day-to-day management + grievance handling + community meeting facilitation; decision-maker: elected board chair).

**Impact layer**
> 1,800 East Bay catchment residents (entire catchment); 70% women-headed households (1,260); 200 children under 5 in the catchment; 80 elderly residents (65+); 12 Persons with Disabilities (Clarendon Health Department records); 1 perennial stream restored supporting 14 downstream smallholder farmers; biodiversity-rich micro-habitat supporting native pollinators + bird species.

### Block 6: Key Resources

**Stewardship & Adoption layer**
> 30-year right-of-use granted by the Clarendon Parish Council for 1.2 hectares (signed Memorandum of Agreement); draft Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with a 3-year budget commitment of JMD $1.2M/year from FY 2027; East Bay Community Trust (legally registered, 7-member elected board, audited financials available).

**Impact layer**
> 480 native seedling inventory (Jamaican guango, lignum vitae, ironwood — sourced from Island Seedling Cooperative); IUCN-aligned technical advisor on retainer (2 days/month); 24 East Bay Community Trust volunteers (signed commitment letters covering 16 hours per month × 24 months); M&E system with quarterly reporting template approved by RADA.

### Block 7: Channels

**Stewardship & Adoption layer**
> Monthly report-in to the Clarendon Parish Council climate committee; quarterly co-design workshop at East Bay Methodist Church; signage at the Greenway entrance with grievance hotline number, Trust contact email, and Parish Council oversight contact.

**Impact layer**
> Site open 24/7 with bilingual community signage (patois + English); school partnership with Riverside Primary for outdoor classroom + monthly tree-planting day; monthly walk-and-talk for elders led by the Trust; quarterly newsletter distributed by community volunteers to all 480 East Bay households; annual public report posted at the parish council with photo evidence + signed community endorsement.

### Block 8: Cost Structure

**Stewardship & Adoption layer**
> Grant capital Year 1: JMD $8.4M (trees + bioswales + signage + tools + training). Post-grant operating (Year 2 onwards) from Clarendon Parish Council adopted budget: JMD $1.2M/year. Community in-kind contribution valued at replacement cost: $1.2M/year (24 volunteers × 16 hours × $300/hour). Administrative: $980K (7%).

**Impact layer**
> MRV: $280K/year. IUCN NbS self-assessment: $100K (Year 1 only). Community engagement: $340K/year. GBV safeguards: $80K. Knowledge products (annual report + newsletter + signage): $50K.

### Block 9: Revenue Streams / Sustainability Inflows

**Stewardship & Adoption layer**
> ★ Clarendon Parish Council Year 2-4 budget line: JMD $1.2M/year (Memorandum of Understanding in drafting). ★ Community in-kind contribution: $1.2M/year (24 volunteers × 16 hours × $300/hour replacement cost). Implicit stormwater-management saving to Parish Council: $2.4M/year (replacing storm-drain clearance, RADA-verified).

**Impact layer**
> Optional Year 3+: blue carbon credits not applicable (terrestrial canopy only); Verra Tree-CO₂ credits projected $400K/year from Year 3 onwards. Parametric insurance payout potential (flood reduction): $200K average per qualifying rainfall event (Jamaica Insurance Association preliminary pricing). Green Climate Fund Simplified Approval Project application Year 2: $2M.

## Final reflection (Stage 6 — fictional learner reflection)

> "I came into this assuming the Public Good pathway meant 'no money story to tell.' That was wrong. The implicit storm-drain saving to the parish council ($2.4M/year) is a real, RADA-verified financial argument for why they should adopt this into their budget. Combined with the in-kind community contribution ($1.2M/year), the inflows actually exceed the operating cost by a comfortable 29% — which is more than I expected. The amber on T2 (no signed MoU yet) is the single most important thing standing between this canvas and a clean PASS. The Adopting Agency Mirage warning was unsettling but correct: a Letter of Intent is not the same thing as a budget commitment in writing."
