## Theory of change

A cooperative of 14 women smallholders in the St. Mary hills establishes a climate-smart apiculture (beekeeping) operation centred on native pollinator-friendly tree species. The cooperative sells honey, propolis, and beeswax products to local hotels and a heritage-tourism trail, generating revenue that funds ongoing operations. The intervention restores 4 hectares of degraded hillside with native flowering trees, increases pollinator biodiversity, reduces erosion risk, and creates 14 full-time + 8 seasonal livelihoods.

## Pathway Engine canvas — populated

### Block 1: Key Partners

**Commercial layer (CVP)**
> Blue Mountain Inn (offtaker — 12-month honey supply contract signed); Heritage Hills Restaurant Group (B2B customer for honey + propolis); Island Seedling Cooperative (supplier of 1,200 native flowering trees); Jamaica Organic Agriculture Movement (certification body). Cane Valley Tourism Co-op (eco-tourism trail partner).

**Impact layer (IVP)**
> Cane Valley Community Association (lead community partner — represents 240 households); RADA extension officer for St. Mary (technical advisor on apiculture); Coastal Conservation Alliance (NGO advising on native species selection); EFJ Programme Officer (funder liaison).

### Block 2: Key Activities

**Commercial layer (CVP)**
> Year-round apiculture (24 hives, scaling to 48 by Month 9); twice-monthly honey harvest; processing + bottling in a shared facility; weekly delivery to Blue Mountain Inn and Heritage Hills (Tuesday and Friday); Saturday eco-trail tours (4 tours/month at JMD $3,500/person); social-media + farmers'-market marketing.

**Impact layer (IVP)**
> Plant 1,200 native flowering trees (mahoe, dogwood, bird-pepper) across 4 hectares of degraded hillside in Year 1; monitor canopy + pollinator counts quarterly; run 4 community co-design workshops with hill-community residents; complete the 5 EFJ-adopted IUCN NbS criteria self-assessment by Month 6; deliver GBV-safe site protocol with referral pathway to St. Mary Police Domestic Violence Intervention Centre.

### Block 3: Value Proposition

**Commercial value proposition**
> Pure single-origin Cane Valley honey at JMD $1,800 per 500ml jar (25% premium over imported honey, traceable to one hillside, certified organic). Heritage Trail eco-tourism experience at JMD $3,500 per person — 3-hour guided walk including honey-tasting, hive observation, and meet-the-beekeepers. Carbon-neutral storyline backed by 1,200 trees planted.

**Impact value proposition**
> Urban-edge ecosystem restoration — 4 hectares of native pollinator-friendly hillside restored (was bare red-soil erosion zone); pollinator population baseline established and tracked; livelihoods for 14 women and 8 seasonal workers; reduced erosion risk for downstream Cane Valley village (population ~600); contribution to Jamaica's Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC) target on terrestrial ecosystem restoration.

### Block 4: Customer / Stewardship Relationship

**Commercial layer**
> Weekly produce list shared by messaging app to all hotel buyers; 12-month signed offtake contract with Blue Mountain Inn (60% of honey volume); quarterly impact stories shared with Blue Mountain marketing team (used in guest welcome packets); annual buyer site-visit + harvest demo.

**Impact layer**
> Cane Valley Community Association co-management committee (4 members, quarterly meetings with signed minutes); annual community report posted at the parish council with photo evidence + signed community endorsement; grievance hotline +1-876-XXX-XXXX with 14-day response standard published on a sign at the apiary.

### Block 5: Customer / Beneficiary Segment

**Commercial layer**
> B2B: 2 hotels in the St. Mary corridor (60% of revenue). B2B restaurant: Heritage Hills Restaurant Group (15%). B2C: 200 weekend market shoppers (10%). Eco-trail tourism: 192 visitors per year at JMD $3,500 (15%). All within a 50-kilometre radius of the apiary.

**Impact layer**
> 240 households in the Cane Valley catchment benefit indirectly (reduced erosion, pollinator support for backyard gardens); 14 women cooperative members (direct livelihoods); 8 seasonal workers (youth, 19-26); 600 downstream village residents (reduced erosion risk); restored native pollinator community supporting 6 km² of adjacent backyard agriculture.

### Block 6: Key Resources

**Commercial layer**
> 4-hectare leased hillside site (12-year right-of-use granted by parish council); 48-hive apiculture infrastructure (24 hives Year 1, scaling); shared honey-processing facility (rented from Cane Valley Co-op); 'Cane Valley Honey' brand registered with the Companies Office; 2 full-time cooperative members + 12 part-time members.

**Impact layer**
> 1,200 native seedling inventory (sourced from Island Seedling Cooperative); IUCN-aligned technical advisor on retainer (2 days/month); community trust built via Cane Valley Community Association's prior 4-year track record; M&E spreadsheet tracking canopy area, pollinator count, beneficiary feedback, and erosion proxy (visible-sediment-after-rain photographs).

### Block 7: Channels

**Commercial layer**
> Hotel deliveries Tuesday + Friday by truck; messaging-app B2B orders for restaurants; Saturday farmers' market stall in St. Mary town centre; Instagram + Facebook page in patois + English for B2C; monthly eco-trail tour bookings via web form.

**Impact layer**
> Community-meeting talks monthly at the village hall; parish radio station feature quarterly (St. Mary FM); printed annual report distributed to 240 catchment households; school-assembly talks twice a year at Cane Valley All-Age School.

### Block 8: Cost Structure

**Commercial layer**
> Capital Year 1: JMD $2.8M (24 hives + safety gear + processing equipment + brand registration). Operating per year: $1.4M (staff + materials + delivery fuel). Administrative: $620K (8% of total) — fully justified per EFJ rule.

**Impact layer**
> MRV (Monitoring, Reporting and Verification): $180K/year. IUCN NbS self-assessment: $80K (Year 1 only). Community engagement: $200K/year (workshops + materials). GBV safeguards: $40K. Knowledge products (annual report + radio features): $50K.

### Block 9: Revenue Streams / Sustainability Inflows

**Commercial layer**
> Honey sales: 60% of inflows = JMD $4.2M/year. Propolis + beeswax: $600K. Eco-trail tourism: $670K. Certification premium (organic price uplift): $300K.

**Impact layer**
> Optional Year 3+: Verra carbon credits (1,200 trees over 25 years) — projected $500K/year from Year 3. In-kind community labour valued at replacement cost: $200K/year. Year 3 follow-on EFJ cycle application (programmed): $1M.
